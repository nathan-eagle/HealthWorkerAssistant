1. Patient Diagnosis
Patient with infected wound from animal bite that occurred February 15 (approximately 2 weeks ago). Wound described as "wide open" with infection. Patient reports medication-induced tremors. Being referred to Rural Health Office (RHO) for evaluation and treatment.

2. Additional Questions
- Animal type that caused the bite
- Wound location, size, and characteristics
- Rabies post-exposure prophylaxis status
- Tetanus vaccination history
- Medication causing tremors and its indication
- Presence of fever or systemic symptoms
- Previous healthcare evaluation of wound
- Home treatments attempted
- Animal's location and rabies observation status
- Signs of infection progression (pain, redness, swelling)
- Underlying conditions affecting wound healing

3. Recommendations
- Urgent RHO referral for wound assessment
- Rabies prophylaxis evaluation based on exposure
- Tetanus prophylaxis consideration
- Proper wound cleaning and dressing
- Antibiotic therapy assessment for infection
- Medication review regarding tremors
- Home wound care instructions
- Documentation of wound characteristics
- Follow-up appointment scheduling
- Animal bite reporting to authorities
- Education on infection warning signs

4. Red Flags and Concerns
- "Wide open" infected wound requiring prompt attention
- Two-week delay in seeking appropriate care
- Unreported tremors potentially indicating medication effect or neurological symptom
- No rabies risk assessment despite animal bite
- Disorganized assessment lacking essential information
- Absence of wound care or infection management plan
- Animal type not identified for rabies risk assessment
- Tetanus status not verified for open wound
- Confused, repetitive communication pattern

5. Cultural Competency Observations
- Appropriate use of respectful term "Nanay" for older woman
- Casual conversation style typical in Filipino healthcare
- Need for clear explanation of rabies prophylaxis importance
- Consideration of RHO access barriers (transportation, cost)
- Respectful approach to potential traditional healing practices
- Verification of understanding needed for follow-up instructions
